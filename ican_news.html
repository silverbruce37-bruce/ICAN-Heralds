Photo: Korean Cultural Center officials and Philippine counterparts launch historic cultural partnership (April 13, 2026)
By ICAN Herald Editorial · 5 min read5분 읽기

The Korean Embassy in Manila and the Philippine Department of Foreign Affairs announced a historic cultural partnership Monday, launching an ambitious year-long initiative aimed at deepening connections between Korea and the Philippines. The program encompasses 50 cultural exchange events spanning music, food, language, and traditional arts, with participation from governmental institutions and private sector organizations. Officials emphasized that this partnership represents people-to-people diplomacy and strengthens bilateral relations beyond commercial interests.

Major components include monthly K-culture festivals in Metro Manila and provincial cities, Korean language scholarship programs for Filipino students, and culinary exchange events featuring both cuisines. The Korean Cultural Center will establish three new satellite offices outside Metro Manila to ensure nationwide reach. Ambassador Park Min-jun stated that this initiative demonstrates Korea's commitment to long-term cultural engagement with the Filipino community, creating educational and cultural pathways for future generations.

The program launches with a grand opening festival at the Cultural Center of the Philippines next weekend, featuring Seoul-based artists and demonstrations by Korean master chefs. Organizers anticipate over 10,000 attendees. The initiative is expected to benefit cultural practitioners, educators, and Korean residents across the country. This comes amid robust Korean business expansion, with over 2,500 Korean companies now operating in the Philippines.

주필리핀 한국대사관과 필리핀 외교부가 월요일 역사적 문화교류 협력을 발표했다. 올해 한필 관계 심화를 목표로 음악, 음식, 언어, 전통예술 등 50개 문화교류행사를 추진한다. 정부기관과 민간기업이 함께 참여하며, 경제 관심을 넘어 국민 간 외교와 양국 관계 강화에 중점을 두고 있다.

Why Korean Barbecue Is How We Heal Together 한국식 구이가 우리가 함께 치유하는 방식인 이유

There's a specific kind of presence that happens around a hot grill. You can't scroll or pretend to listen—the moment demands your attention. The meat sizzles, the side dishes multiply, and suddenly everyone at the table becomes part of the same ritual. This week, our food feature takes us to Gogi House in BGC, where a Seoul restaurateur has recreated not just a restaurant, but a gathering place that feels like family.뜨거운 그릴 주위에서 일어나는 특정한 종류의 존재가 있습니다. 당신은 스크롤하거나 귀를 기울이는 척 할 수 없습니다—순간은 당신의 주의를 요구합니다. 고기가 지글지글 타고, 반찬이 늘어나고, 갑자기 테이블의 모든 사람이 같은 의식의 일부가 됩니다. 이 주, 우리의 음식 피처는 서울 레스토랑 주인이 단순한 레스토랑뿐만 아니라 가족처럼 느껴지는 모임 장소를 재현한 BGC의 고기 하우스로 데려갑니다.

Korean barbecue isn't efficient. It's slow. You wait for meat to cook, you build bites with your hands, you share the same grill with people next to you. In a city that moves as fast as Manila, this deliberateness feels revolutionary—a rebellion against productivity culture disguised as dinner. The owner once told me, 'Every meal here should feel like celebrating with family,' and I realized he wasn't talking about blood relations.
